Chapter I

World & Setting

An eleven-node region, a quiet corporate conspiracy, and seven factions tugging at the same thread.

Status of this Chapter

§1 — The Region

The campaign takes place inside a node-based region of eleven named locations. Travel is point-to-point along marked routes; spaces between are wilderness. §2 — The Two-Tier Narrative

The campaign has two reading levels. Players see Tier 1 from the start; Tier 2 unlocks via the CK questline. Both tiers are canon simultaneously. Tier 2 does not retcon Tier 1 — it reveals what was always true.

Tier 1 — Surface Story

The Pokémon League is under strain. The CK Gym Leader has refused League authority and is associated with the Anarchist movement. HAPPINY Farms is a globally trusted non-profit operating every Pokémon Center on the planet, and it runs a respected "retirement" program for released Pokémon. The League is planning two new Gyms — Grass at TD, Fire at TJ — to round out regional coverage. Foreign Delegates have arrived at Bluff City, investigating something diplomatic.

Tier 2 — The Hidden Truth

The retirement program is euthanization. Pokémon Centers are the intake funnel. Every release in this region is a death sentence. The pipeline runs Centers → Film Industry "retirement" referrals → HAPPINY Farm near TB → quiet disposal. The Foreign Delegates are not investigating the League; they are investigating HAPPINY. The two planned Gyms are HAPPINY-funded: TD's Grass Gym is meant to close the Foreign Delegate investigation, and TJ's Fire Gym is meant to destroy the CK sanctuary once the crossroads clears. The Anarchist movement may have known longer than anyone.

§4 — Plot Mechanics

Spirit Walk
Learned after defeating the Ghost Gym at CK. Allows trainers and Pokémon to pass invisibly through certain gates and to enter the dimensional-bleed layer. Humans risk losing their spirit to the Ghost Realm — recovery is a side quest. Pokémon are unaffected. Core mechanic for the Channeler archetype's Secret Power trait.
Spinda Gate
The direct CH↔CI route is locked behind a daily-changing Spinda pattern key. Acquire a Spinda from Spinda Win Casino in CE; match its pattern to today's lock. Designed to force players through CE (and thus discover the Film Industry plot) before they can reach the Fairy Gym at CI.
Channeler Rumor System
Channeler trainer encounters reward rumors instead of money. Rumors are fragments in three categories — Landmark (where), Observation (what kind of thing), Event (why it matters) — that collapse into specific anomaly sites when investigated together. Higher-tier rewards: Map Location, Dimensional Bleed Site, Safari Ticket.

Chapter II

Pokémon: Species, Stats & Roles

Where the creatures come from, how their numbers are derived, and how the table reads what kit a species brings.

Status of this Chapter

  • Completed: Stat conversion formula (HP÷2.5, others÷10, ceiling, no minimum); 1d6 variance; 25 Natures; six combat roles with stat triads; dynamic role detection from raw stats; four-stage evolution numbering (0/1/2/3); Option C dice tiers adopted (Stage 1 = 3d8); forms vs stages schema (forms is the outer wrapper, stages nest inside).
  • In progress: Migrating master-dex.json to raw-stat storage; removing the old defaultRole field; expanding the bulk-import tool; growing the dex past 28 entries.
  • Planned: Per-species catchThreshold and rarityClass fields; full 151 Kanto coverage; Johto and Hoenn additions; optional "build role" classifier that respects EVs and Nature.

§1 — Stat Conversion

Every Pokémon's playable stats are derived from its raw game-base stats at the moment of capture. The conversion formula is the same in every tool, on every sheet, forever.

For HP:

⌈ raw_HP ÷ 2.5 ⌉

For ATK, DEF, S.ATK, S.DEF, SPD:

⌈ raw_stat ÷ 10 ⌉

No minimum is enforced. A weak stat is allowed to be very low — that is intentional; the system tolerates a 1 or even a 0.

Variance (one-time, permanent)

At capture, roll 1d6 for each of the six stats and add the rolls to the converted base. These six rolls are permanent. They are written on the Pokémon's sheet and never re-rolled — not on evolution, not on EV gain, not on level up. They are the species' personality in numbers.

Worked Example — Bulbasaur

§2 — Natures

There are 25 Natures. Twenty boost one stat by +2 and reduce another by −2; the remaining five are neutral (no modifier). Nature is assigned randomly at encounter. Nature never modifies HP, even if the table below would suggest it could.

§3 — The Six Roles

Role is determined by which of six stat triads scores highest on the Pokémon's raw stats. (Raw, not converted — the conversion formula amplifies HP so much that on converted stats the Defender triad would win for almost every species, which is wrong.) Role describes what the species brings to a team; it never gates moves or abilities. Nature and EVs do not change role.

§4 — Stages & Evolution

Evolution uses a four-tier numbering: Stage 0 (baby), Stage 1 (base), Stage 2 (mid), Stage 3 (final). Most lines start at Stage 1; babies exist only for species that received pre-evolutions in later generations. Stage numbering governs which dice tier the Pokémon uses (see Chapter V).

On evolution, recompute converted stats using the new stage's raw values, add the original d6 variance rolls, then re-apply the same Nature. Dice pool refreshes fully at the new tier — all permanently lost dice are recovered. Evolution is the comeback mechanic.

§5 — Forms

Forms (Kanto/Alola/Galar/Hisui/Paldea variants) are the outermost wrapper in the data schema; stages nest inside forms. A species with one form still uses the wrapper — there is exactly one form. A species with regional variants has one form per variant, each with its own stages. This means Rattata-Kanto and Rattata-Alola are two forms under one species entry, not two species entries.

Chapter III

Trainers & Archetypes

No class at creation. Players earn their archetype by playing it.

Status of this Chapter

  • Completed: Six archetypes with two characteristic traits each; the Fixed vs Scaling trait distinction; Trainer Points / Badge Points / Rare Candy economy; synergy passive concept; Socialite's unique rule-break (Status on Standard moves).
  • In progress: Ranger skill tree branches A and B; balancing the rate at which Trainer Points accrue per archetype-aligned action.
  • Planned: Per-tier skill lists for every archetype; trainer character sheet as a printable HTML form; the Channeler rumor reward tables; Burglar contextual loot tables expanded per region; full archetype synergy matrix.

§1 — Trait Types

Each archetype offers two Characteristic Traits, one per branch. A trait is either Fixed (powerful and static) or Scaling (narrow at first, deepens with engagement). Branch A is available from start; Branch B unlocks with sufficient investment.

§2 — The Six Archetypes

Ace Trainer — Combat Mastery

  • Tactics (Scaling). Overworld: predicts opponent strategy from tells; reads Gym Leader tendencies. Combat: positioning, initiative manipulation, multi-Pokémon coordination. Deepens with battle experience.
  • Item Research (Scaling). Overworld: identifies item properties and synergies; may unlock non-standard items. Combat: held-item deployment and consumable optimization. Deep investment unlocks unique interactions.

Ranger — Wilderness & Survival

  • Wild Attunement (Scaling). Overworld: navigates terrain and weather; identifies attractants/repellents; tracks wildlife. Combat: passively shapes weather conditions in a matching biome; reduces jungle Exposure.
  • Favored Type (Scaling). Overworld: improved catch rates for favored type; unlocks additional types over time. Combat: bonuses commanding the favored type in lane and jungle. Attunement + Favored synergy → Super Effective triggers.

Socialite — Socialization & Faction Navigation

  • Fashionable (Fixed). Overworld: advantages in beauty contests, high-status NPC interactions, faction first impressions. Combat: passive deference and attraction effects on opponents and minions.
  • Status (Fixed). Overworld: social status as leverage — negotiation, faction reputation, high-level info. Combat: unique rule-break — Standard moves can cause status effects. The only such break in the system.

Burglar — Sabotage & Infiltration

  • Swipe (Fixed). Overworld: sleight of hand, pickpocketing, physical security bypass. Combat: steal held items or disrupt opponent item economy mid-battle.
  • Stealth (Scaling). Overworld: infiltration, restricted-area movement, Spirit Walk without alerting wards. Combat: ambush setup; reduces enemy awareness before a match.

Tamer — Pokémon Husbandry

  • Rearing (Scaling). Overworld: breeding, egg hatching, EV investment, long-term Pokémon development. Combat: unlocks moveset expansion — deep investment enables moves normally unavailable to a species.
  • Whisperer (Scaling). Overworld: calms wild or distressed Pokémon; manages obedience for high-level catches. Combat: reduces disobedience risk for Pokémon exceeding the trainer's level threshold. Synergizes with Socialite Status.

Channeler — Weird & Dimensional

  • Witness (Scaling). Overworld: foresight, divination, reading anomaly sites; identifies dimensional bleed locations. Combat: reduces surprise vulnerability in jungle; can read opponent intent before commits.
  • Secret Power (Scaling). Overworld: perceives Ghost-type Pokémon invisible to others; enables Spirit Walk; teleportation at advanced tiers. Combat: manifests through Pokémon rather than the trainer. Witness + Secret Power synergy: finds anomalies and enters them.

§3 — Trainer Progression

Trainer Points are earned from archetype-aligned actions (each archetype has its own list). Badge Points accrue from every Gym milestone and may be spent anywhere — these are the "Rare Candy" of trainer progression. Skill tiers unlock at corresponding badge counts: Tier 1 at Badge 1, Tier 2 at Badge 2, and so on. There is no hard gating between trees; investing in multiple archetypes is normal and supported by the synergy passives.

Chapter IV

The Battle Round

Four phases per round, each touching every active Pokémon, minion, and trainer.

Status of this Chapter

  • Completed: Four-phase round structure (Roll / Action / Minion Wave / Upkeep); initiative formula; the win conditions for both players and Gym Leaders.
  • In progress: A reference card showing the per-phase checklist for the GM to read aloud; balancing the upkeep tick rate of status conditions.
  • Planned: Multi-arena coordination rules (when two parallel lanes share a round); the boss-phase variant for Gym Leader Mega encounters.

§1 — Phase Sequence

  1. Roll Phase. Every active Pokémon (player-side and NPC-side) rolls fresh dice for the round, using its per-stage roll (see Chapter V). Banked dice stay banked — do not touch them. Minions do not roll; their behavior is deterministic.
  2. Action Phase. In initiative order, each trainer allocates that Pokémon's dice. Allocations include: spend on a move (Standard, Special, Signature, or Struggle), bank for later, or commit to jungle (Movement / Action / Stability — see Chapter VI).
  3. Minion Wave Phase. The wave resolves movement and attacks. Three units of any tier merge into one unit of the next tier. New minions arrive on the Global XP escalator (Chapter VII).
  4. Upkeep Phase. Status effects tick. XP is awarded. Evolution thresholds are checked; eligible Pokémon evolve immediately and refresh their pool at the new tier.

§2 — Initiative

Initiative this round:

initiative = (sum of this round's rolled energy dice) + SPD + modifiers

The dice sum has a natural floor that rises with evolution: 2d12 minimum 2, 3d8 minimum 3, 4d6 minimum 4, 6d4 minimum 6. The ceiling is 24 at every tier (2×12 = 3×8 = 4×6 = 6×4). Modifiers come from held items, move priority effects, status conditions, and trainer abilities.

Highest initiative acts first. Ties break on SPD first, then on GM discretion. This replaces the older "+100 priority brackets / highest fresh die" formula that lived in the rulebook page — that version is non-canon.

§3 — Win Conditions

Players win by:

  1. Clearing the Gym's typed Obstacle (requires the HM skill earned from the Obstacle itself);
  2. Defeating all four of the Gym Leader's field Pokémon;
  3. Defeating the Boss — the Mega Evolution of the Gym Leader's signature Pokémon.

Gym Leaders win by:

Attritioning player Pokémon dice pools to the point of non-contest. Gym Leaders have no Nexus to destroy — their entire strategy is endurance.

§3 — The Selection Model

This is the one rule players miss most often, so it gets its own paragraph. Dice for any move are chosen from both the fresh pool and the bank. Banked dice are not "locked up" — they are accumulated energy a player can spend at any time. Standard moves require at least one selected die (any source). Special and Signature require the selected dice to sum to at least the move's energy threshold. The bank acts as long-term storage; the fresh pool acts as this-round's pickup; the player mixes from both.

§4 — Damage Formulas

Standard move damage (per die spent):

damage = max(1, die_value + ATK − DEF + type_mod)

Sum across all selected dice. Each die is rolled individually through the formula, so spending three dice yields three separate damage events that are then summed.

Special / Signature damage:

damage = max(1, sum(selected_die_values) + S.ATK − S.DEF + type_mod)

A single calculation, using the sum of the selected dice. Banked dice preserve the values they were banked at — they are not re-rolled. Banking a [12, 10, 8] and later spending it on a Special gives 30 + S.ATK − S.DEF + type_mod, not a fresh roll.

Struggle:

damage = 4 (to both Pokémon); consumes no dice

Always available. Designed so a Pokémon that has somehow lost its entire pool can still take action and bring the fight to a conclusion.

Chapter VI

The Jungle Layer

A non-spatial layer where Pokémon roam unsupervised, gathering resources and risk in parallel to the lane.

Status of this Chapter

  • Completed: The three resource axes (Reach, Exposure, Stability); the three encounter categories (Hunt, Pressure, Ambush); role-by-role jungle fitness profile.
  • In progress: Concrete Reach gates (Low/Mid/High/Extreme thresholds in playable numbers); Exposure breakpoint that triggers a forced jungler collision.
  • Planned: Per-biome jungle table; weather effects on jungle accumulation; Ambush window cards (one per Pokémon role).

§1 — Reach & Exposure

The trainer never enters the jungle directly. Pokémon roam there, accumulating two resources:

Reach
Built by allocating dice to Movement in the Action phase. Reach gates encounter tier — Low at 1+, Mid at 2+, High at 3+, Extreme at 4+. Reach resets at the end of each match.
Exposure
Accumulated by risky actions: Hunt encounters, certain Ambush windows, high-tier movement without supporting Stability. Exposure never decays during a match. When two opposing junglers' combined Exposure crosses a threshold, the GM triggers a forced collision.
Stability
Built by allocating dice to Stability in the Action phase. Stability counters Exposure accumulation, keeping a fragile jungler alive.

§2 — Jungle Encounter Types

  • Hunt. Direct combat with wild Pokémon. ATK-reliant. The primary source of personal jungle XP.
  • Pressure. Special-attack-based action. Triggers global map effects, applying lane pressure from off-screen even when no jungler is in lane.
  • Ambush. Sets traps for opposing junglers or ganks into lane. Requires Reach and committed investment up front.

§2 — Escalation

Every 30 combined Global XP earned by the players, one new Tier-1 minion is added to the wave permanently. If any tier then has more than three units, three of them merge into one of the next tier. Merges cascade — a T1 merge that produces a fourth T2 then merges into a T3 in the same upkeep.

§3 — Field Behemoth

When three T3 minions exist simultaneously, a Field Behemoth spawns automatically at a defined map position. This is not GM discretion — it fires when the condition is met. The Behemoth is a neutral boss, visible to all players, and represents the primary match-ending pressure mechanism for stalled games. Defeating it grants substantial team XP and a one-time team-wide buff for the remainder of the match.

Chapter VIII

Roll under. Better balls roll fewer dice. The math always favors the prepared trainer.

§1 — The Core Mechanic

To catch a Pokémon, the player rolls dice (determined by the ball used) and compares the sum to a Catch Threshold derived for that encounter. If the sum is under the threshold, the catch succeeds. Rolling high is bad. Better Poké Balls roll fewer dice, making low sums more predictable.

Chapter IX

Economy, Experience & Evolution

How XP flows, what money fights are worth, and when a Pokémon levels up to its next stage.

Status of this Chapter

  • Completed: Global vs Personal XP split; minion XP awards; evolution thresholds (30 / 70 Global XP); the trainer payout formula and its archetype scalars; Burglar contextual loot table; Gym prize money formula; Channeler rumor reward concept.
  • In progress: Playtest calibration of evolution timing; tuning the rate of Global XP per minion clear so that evolution lands on the intended round.
  • Planned: EV award rates per encounter type; Tier-2 unlock specials that grant special loot tables; team-wide consumables economy.

§1 — Experience

Global XP (team-wide)

  • Minion spawn: +1 per minion.
  • Minion cleared: +1 per T1, +3 per T2, +9 per T3.
  • Obstacle destroyed: +20.

Personal XP (per trainer)

  • KO Stage 0 Pokémon: +3.
  • KO Stage 1 Pokémon: +4.
  • KO Stage 2 Pokémon: +5.

§2 — Evolution Thresholds

  • To Stage 1 (from Stage 0): Global XP ≥ 30.
  • To Stage 2 (from Stage 1): Global XP ≥ 30.
  • To Stage 3 (from Stage 2): Global XP ≥ 70.

Evolution triggers a full pool refresh at the new stage size. All permanently lost dice are recovered. This is the deliberate comeback mechanic.

§3 — Trainer Battle Payouts

Money awarded to the players for a won trainer battle:

P = A × ∑( ⌈ L ÷ 10 ⌉ × 100 )

A = archetype scalar of the defeated trainer. L = individual level of each Pokémon in their party. ∑ = sum across all of their Pokémon before the scalar multiplies through.

Appendix B

Hand-Runnable Algorithms

Every PKTTRPG tool that lives in code has a paper twin here. If the computer is unavailable, every system in this manual can still be run with dice, pencil, and the tables in Appendix A.

B.1 — Roll Dice (rollDice)

Purpose: Replaces rollDice(notation) from shared/js/stats.js. Given a string like "3d8", produces a list of individual die values.

PROCEDURE Roll-Dice (notation)
  1. Read the notation. Split it on the letter "d".
     Call the number before "d" the COUNT.
     Call the number after "d" the SIDES.
  2. Make an empty list called RESULTS.
  3. Repeat COUNT times:
       a. Roll one die with SIDES faces.
       b. Write the result into RESULTS.
  4. Return RESULTS.

EXAMPLE  Roll-Dice("3d8")
  COUNT = 3, SIDES = 8.
  Roll three d8s: say 5, 2, 7.
  Return [5, 2, 7]. (Do NOT sum unless a later step says to.)

B.2 — Convert a Single Stat (convertStat)

Purpose: Replaces convertStat(raw, isHP).

PROCEDURE Convert-Stat (raw, is-HP?)
  1. If is-HP? is TRUE:
       Divide raw by 2.5.
     Otherwise:
       Divide raw by 10.
  2. Round the result UP (the "ceiling" — any fractional part rounds up).
  3. Return the rounded value. Do NOT apply any minimum.

EXAMPLE  Convert-Stat(45, is-HP? = TRUE)
  45 ÷ 2.5 = 18.0.  Round up: 18.  Return 18.

EXAMPLE  Convert-Stat(49, is-HP? = FALSE)
  49 ÷ 10 = 4.9.   Round up: 5.   Return 5.

EXAMPLE  Convert-Stat(3, is-HP? = FALSE)
  3 ÷ 10 = 0.3.    Round up: 1.   Return 1.
  (For raw values 4 to 9, the ceiling also gives 1. That is correct.)

Or just use Table A.2 / A.3 for instant lookups.

B.3 — Apply a Nature (applyNature)

Purpose: Replaces applyNature(stats, nature).

PROCEDURE Apply-Nature (stats, nature)
  1. If the nature is one of the five neutral natures
     (Hardy, Docile, Serious, Bashful, Quirky):
       Do nothing. Return stats unchanged.
  2. Look up the nature's BOOST stat and REDUCE stat in Table A.4.
  3. If BOOST is anything other than HP:
       Add +2 to that stat on the sheet.
  4. If REDUCE is anything other than HP:
       Subtract −2 from that stat on the sheet.
     (HP is NEVER modified by a Nature, regardless of the table.)
  5. Return the modified stats.

EXAMPLE  Apply-Nature(stats, Jolly)
  Jolly: BOOST = SPD, REDUCE = S.ATK.
  Add +2 to SPD; subtract 2 from S.ATK.
  HP untouched.

B.4 — Generate a Pokémon (generateStats)

Purpose: Replaces generateStats(rawStats, nature). This is what the in-game Pokédex Generator tool does on the website.

PROCEDURE Generate-Stats (raw-stats, optional nature)
  1. Roll a Nature if one was not provided:
       Roll 1d25 (or 1d100 ÷ 4, round up) and read Table A.4.
  2. For each of the six stats (HP, ATK, DEF, S.ATK, S.DEF, SPD):
       a. Convert it with Convert-Stat (B.2).
          HP uses ÷2.5; the others use ÷10. Always round up. No floor.
       b. Roll 1d6.
       c. Add the d6 result to the converted stat.
       d. Write BOTH the final stat AND the d6 result on the sheet.
          (The d6 result is the "variance" and is PERMANENT — it never
           changes, not even on evolution.)
  3. Apply the Nature (B.3) to the resulting stats.
  4. Done. The sheet now shows the playable stats.

EXAMPLE  Generate-Stats(Bulbasaur raw, Jolly)
  Raw:        HP 45,  ATK 49, DEF 49, S.ATK 65, S.DEF 65, SPD 45
  Converted:  HP 18,  ATK 5,  DEF 5,  S.ATK 7,  S.DEF 7,  SPD 5
  d6 rolls:        4       2       3        1         2       5
  After +d6:  HP 22,  ATK 7,  DEF 8,  S.ATK 8,  S.DEF 9,  SPD 10
  Apply Jolly (+SPD, −S.ATK):
              HP 22,  ATK 7,  DEF 8,  S.ATK 6,  S.DEF 9,  SPD 12

B.5 — Re-derive on Evolution (reapplyOnEvolution)

Purpose: Replaces reapplyOnEvolution. Same d6 rolls; new stage's raw stats; same Nature.

PROCEDURE Re-Apply-On-Evolution (new-raw-stats, saved-d6-rolls, nature)
  1. For each of the six stats:
       a. Convert the NEW raw stat (B.2).
       b. Add the SAVED d6 roll from the original capture.
          (Do not roll a new d6. The d6 rolls are the Pokémon's permanent
           personality and never change.)
  2. Apply the Nature (B.3) to the resulting stats.
  3. Refresh the dice pool to the new stage's tier (Table A.5).
     All previously lost dice are recovered.
  4. Done.

B.6 — Detect Role (detectRole)

Purpose: Replaces detectRole(rawStats). Run on RAW stats, not converted ones.

PROCEDURE Detect-Role (raw-stats)
  1. Using Table A.7, compute six sums on the species' RAW stats:
       Defender    = HP   + DEF  + S.DEF
       All-Rounder = ATK  + SPD  + DEF
       Attacker    = S.ATK+ SPD  + S.DEF
       Speedster   = ATK  + S.ATK+ SPD
       Controller  = ATK  + DEF  + S.DEF
       Supporter   = S.ATK+ DEF  + S.DEF
  2. The role with the highest sum is the role.
  3. If two sums tie, the GM picks based on the species' flavor.

WHY RAW AND NOT CONVERTED?
  PKTTRPG conversion divides HP by 2.5 and other stats by 10.
  Converted HP is therefore 3–4× larger than other converted stats.
  Summing converted stats would make the Defender triad (HP+DEF+S.DEF)
  win for almost every species, which is wrong. On raw stats HP is still
  usually the biggest of the six, but it does not dominate — bulky
  species lean Defender, glass cannons lean Speedster, and so on.

B.7 — Standard Move Damage (damageStandard)

Purpose: Replaces damageStandard(dieValue, atk, def, typeMod).

PROCEDURE Standard-Damage (die-value, attacker-ATK, defender-DEF, type-mod)
  1. Compute: die-value + attacker-ATK − defender-DEF + type-mod
  2. If the result is less than 1, use 1 instead. (Minimum 1 damage.)
  3. Return the result.

NOTE ON MULTIPLE DICE
  If the attacker spent two or more dice on Standard, run this
  procedure ONCE PER SELECTED DIE, then sum the per-die results.

EXAMPLE
  Two dice spent: a 7 and a 5.  ATK 6, DEF 5, type-mod 0.
  Per die: max(1, 7+6-5+0) = 8;  max(1, 5+6-5+0) = 6.
  Total damage = 8 + 6 = 14.

B.8 — Special / Signature Damage (damageSpecial)

Purpose: Replaces damageSpecial(selectedDice, satk, sdef, typeMod).

PROCEDURE Special-Damage (selected-dice, attacker-S.ATK, defender-S.DEF, type-mod)
  1. Verify the move can fire:
       Sum the values of the SELECTED dice (from fresh and/or bank).
       If this sum is less than the move's energy threshold,
       the move cannot fire. Stop.
  2. Compute: sum-of-selected + attacker-S.ATK − defender-S.DEF + type-mod
  3. If the result is less than 1, use 1 instead.
  4. Apply Special / Signature pool effects:
       Special: remove 1 die from the pool temporarily.
       Signature: remove 2 dice from the pool temporarily.
  5. Consume the spent dice (remove them from fresh and/or bank).
  6. Return the damage.

EXAMPLE
  Selected: [6 from bank, 3 from fresh]. Threshold 6. S.ATK 7, S.DEF 5.
  Sum = 9, which meets the threshold.
  Damage = max(1, 9 + 7 - 5 + 0) = 11.

B.9 — Struggle

Purpose: Anti-soft-lock fallback. Always available.

PROCEDURE Struggle (attacker, defender)
  1. The attacker takes 4 damage.
  2. The defender takes 4 damage.
  3. No dice are consumed. No type modifier applies.

B.10 — Type Modifier (typeMod)

Purpose: Replaces typeMod(attackType, defenderTypes, chart).

PROCEDURE Type-Modifier (attack-type, defender-types)
  1. Start with MODIFIER = 0 and IMMUNE = false.
  2. For each of the defender's types:
       a. Look up: attack-type vs that defender-type in the type chart.
       b. If the cell is "immune," set IMMUNE = true and stop the loop.
       c. If the cell is "super effective," add +2 to MODIFIER.
       d. If the cell is "not very effective," subtract 2 from MODIFIER.
       e. If the cell is "neutral," do nothing.
  3. If IMMUNE is true, the attack deals 0 damage — skip the damage
     formula entirely.
  4. Otherwise return MODIFIER.

NOTE ON DUAL TYPES
  Two types can stack: a Fire attack on a Grass+Bug defender adds +2 twice,
  yielding +4 (whether to cap at +2 is in Appendix C — Pending). Until
  Pending Reconciliation resolves, the GM may use either the stacked sum
  or the rulebook's "+3 dual-effective / ±0 cancel" rule, but be consistent.

B.11 — Initiative

Purpose: Replaces initiative(rolledDice, spd, modifiers).

PROCEDURE Initiative (rolled-dice-this-round, SPD, modifiers)
  1. Sum the values of the dice rolled this round.
  2. Add SPD (already converted, with Nature and EV applied).
  3. Add any modifiers from held items, move priorities, status, etc.
  4. Return the total. The Pokémon with the highest initiative acts first.
  5. Ties: break on SPD first, then on GM discretion.

EXAMPLE (Stage 1, rolled [3, 7, 5] on 3d8; SPD 12; +1 from held item)
  Sum dice: 15. Add SPD: 27. Add held item: 28.  Initiative this round: 28.

B.12 — 1v1 Battle Round (the Simulator)

Purpose: Replaces the entire battle/simulator-1v1.html tool. This is the complete turn flow.

PROCEDURE Battle-Round (each Pokémon in play)
  ROLL PHASE
    1. Each Pokémon rolls its per-stage Roll/Round (Table A.5).
       The dice are placed in the "fresh pool" beside the sheet.
       The "bank" (from previous rounds) is untouched.

  INITIATIVE
    2. For each Pokémon, run procedure B.11 using the fresh dice.
       Order the Pokémon by initiative, highest first.

  ACTION PHASE  (in initiative order)
    3. The trainer of the acting Pokémon picks dice to use.
       Dice may come FROM EITHER the fresh pool OR the bank — mix freely.
    4. The trainer declares one of:

       (a) STANDARD MOVE  (e.g. Tackle)
           - Requires at least 1 selected die.
           - Damage: run B.7 once per selected die, then sum.
           - Selected dice are CONSUMED (removed from pool/bank).
           - No temporary pool penalty.

       (b) SPECIAL MOVE  (e.g. Ember)
           - Requires sum(selected dice) ≥ move's energy threshold.
           - Damage: run B.8.
           - Selected dice are CONSUMED.
           - Temporarily remove 1 additional die from pool.

       (c) SIGNATURE MOVE  (e.g. Hydro Pump)
           - Requires sum(selected dice) ≥ move's (usually higher) threshold.
           - Damage: run B.8.
           - Selected dice are CONSUMED.
           - Temporarily remove 2 additional dice from pool.

       (d) BANK
           - Move some or all selected fresh dice into the bank.
           - Cannot exceed the bank cap (Table A.5).
           - Banked dice preserve their face values.

       (e) STRUGGLE  (always available)
           - Run B.9. Both Pokémon take 4 damage. Nothing is consumed.

       (f) END TURN
           - Do nothing this round. Pass to the next combatant.

  MINION-WAVE PHASE
    5. (If minions are present) Resolve wave per Chapter VII rules.

  UPKEEP PHASE
    6. Tick any status conditions on each Pokémon.
    7. Award Global XP and Personal XP per Chapter IX § 1.
    8. Check evolution thresholds. Any Pokémon at threshold evolves
       NOW: re-derive its stats with B.5 and refresh the pool to the
       new tier (Table A.5).
    9. Begin a new round.

ANTI-SOFT-LOCK GUARANTEE
  If a Pokémon ever has zero usable dice in both fresh and bank, it can
  still STRUGGLE every round. Combat will end in finite time.

B.13 — Random Pokémon Generator (the Generator)

Purpose: Replaces pokedex/generator.html.

PROCEDURE Generate-Random-Pokémon
  1. Choose a species and stage (GM call or 1d151 for Kanto + lookup).
  2. Look up that species' raw base stats from the Pokédex source.
  3. Roll a Nature (1d25 → Table A.4).
  4. Run B.4 (Generate-Stats) with the raw stats and the Nature.
  5. Run B.6 to detect role from raw stats and write it on the sheet.
  6. Look up the dice pool for the stage (Table A.5).
  7. Note: starting fresh and bank pools are both empty until battle.

B.14 — Capture Roll Resolution

Purpose: Replaces the JS in rulebook/capture.html.

PROCEDURE Capture-Attempt (ball, target Pokémon, HP-state, status, rarity-class)
  1. If the target is fainted, the capture fails. (Uncatchable.)
  2. Compute the THRESHOLD:
       THRESHOLD = base-threshold-for-species
                 + HP-state-modifier      (Table 8.2)
                 + status-modifier        (Table 8.2)
                 + rarity-class-modifier  (Table 8.3)
     The base-threshold for a species is its "standard difficulty"
     before any state. Until per-species values exist, use 10 as a
     working default for unfamiliar wild Pokémon.
  3. Determine BALL DICE (Table 8.1):
       Poké Ball  = 3d6
       Great Ball = 2d6
       Ultra Ball = 1d6
       Master Ball = automatic catch — skip to step 6.
  4. Roll the ball dice. Sum them.
  5. If the SUM is LESS THAN the THRESHOLD, the catch succeeds.
     If the SUM is EQUAL or GREATER, the catch fails.
  6. On success, the Pokémon is captured. Run B.13 (or B.4) to
     finalize its sheet with Nature, variance, and role.

EXAMPLE (Poké Ball on a half-HP, Paralysis, Uncommon target,
         base difficulty 10)
  Threshold = 10 + 4 (half HP) + 3 (paralysis) − 2 (uncommon) = 15.
  Roll 3d6: say 4 + 5 + 1 = 10. 10 < 15, so the catch succeeds.

B.15 — Trainer Battle Payout

Purpose: Replaces the JS in rulebook/economy.html.

PROCEDURE Compute-Payout (defeated-trainer)
  1. Look up the defeated trainer's archetype scalar A (Table 9.1).
  2. For each Pokémon in their party:
       Compute L = level. Divide L by 10 and ROUND UP. Multiply by 100.
       Call this the per-Pokémon contribution.
  3. SUM the per-Pokémon contributions.
  4. Multiply the sum by A.
  5. Result is the money the players win.

EXAMPLE  (Defeated a Tamer with party: levels 22, 14, 30)
  A = 3.
  Per-Pokémon:  ⌈22/10⌉ × 100 = 300
                ⌈14/10⌉ × 100 = 200
                ⌈30/10⌉ × 100 = 300
  Sum = 800.   Payout = 800 × 3 = $2,400.

B.16 — Minion Wave Escalation

Purpose: Replaces any wave-escalation logic.

PROCEDURE Escalate-Wave (current wave, new Global XP)
  1. Track the team's combined Global XP (Chapter IX).
  2. Every time Global XP crosses a multiple of 30:
       a. Add one new T1 minion to the wave.
       b. After adding, check every tier (T1, T2, T3):
            - If any tier has MORE THAN three units,
              remove three of them and add one unit of the next tier.
            - If T3 has more than three, the next tier is "Field Behemoth."
              Per Chapter VII, when three T3s exist simultaneously,
              a Field Behemoth spawns automatically.
       c. Cascade merges — keep checking until no tier has more than three.
  3. Done. Resume play.

B.17 — Sprite Path Lookup

Purpose: Replaces spritePath from shared/js/sprites.js. This is for tables that print a Pokémon's picture from a binder; the algorithm tells the GM which sheet to flip to.

PROCEDURE Find-Sprite (id, optional form-name, optional mega-flag, optional mega-variant)
  1. Drop any leading zeros from the id. ("001" → "1", "025" → "25".)
  2. If the Pokémon is in MEGA form:
       a. If a mega variant is specified ("X" or "Y"), look up
          {id}-mega-{variant}.png  (e.g. 6-mega-x.png).
       b. Otherwise look up {id}-mega.png.
       Stop.
  3. If a form name is given AND the form is NOT a regional variant:
       Look up {id}-{form-name-lowercased-with-hyphens}.png
       (e.g. 25-cosplay.png; 201-a.png for Unown letter A).
       Stop.
  4. Otherwise look up the base sheet: {id}.png.
  5. If the looked-up sheet doesn't exist in the binder, fall back to
     the base sheet.

NOTE — REGIONAL VARIANTS
  Kanto, Alola, Galar, Hisui, Paldea, etc. fall through to the base sheet
  unless the binder includes a dedicated regional sheet.

Appendix D

Glossary

Bank / Banking
Moving fresh dice into a persistent storage area between rounds. Banked dice preserve their values and can be selected for any move later, mixed with fresh dice if desired.
Catch Threshold
The number a capture roll must come in under to succeed. Modified by HP state, status, and rarity.
Exhausted Dice
Dice permanently lost from a Pokémon's pool (via Fainting or voluntary Return to Base). Recovered only on evolution.
Field Behemoth
A neutral boss that spawns automatically when three Tier-3 minions exist simultaneously.
Fresh Dice
Dice rolled this round, available for any allocation (Standard, Special, Signature, banking, or jungle).
Gym Leader
NPC opponent who wins by attrition. Gym Leader Pokémon do not permanently lose dice.
Jungle
Non-spatial layer where Pokémon accumulate Reach and Exposure remotely; covered in Chapter VI.
Minion Wave
Scaling enemy force that grows through the three-into-one merge mechanic on every 30 Global XP.
Nature
One of 25 personality types; applies +2 to one stat and −2 to another (five are neutral). Never modifies HP.
Pool
The total number of dice a Pokémon has available across the entire battle. Refreshes fully on evolution.
Reach
Jungle resource that gates encounter difficulty. Resets between matches.
Role
Classification of a Pokémon based on its highest-summing stat triad. Six roles total.
Spirit Walk
Mechanic learned at the Ghost Gym (CK) that allows invisible passage through certain gates. Required for the Channeler's Secret Power trait.
Struggle
Anti-soft-lock move. Always available. Deals 4 damage to both Pokémon. Consumes no dice.
Tier 1 / Tier 2
Campaign narrative split. Tier 1 is the visible story; Tier 2 is the hidden HAPPINY-conspiracy truth. Both are canon simultaneously.
